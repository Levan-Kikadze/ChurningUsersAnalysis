Observations & Take‑aways

  • First‑order experience is the weakest. One‑time churners suffer the worst operational performance: longest ETA error (‑200 s), highest late‑delivery rate (34 %), and the largest share of “BAD” orders (13 %). More than 60 % of their bad orders are purely 15‑minute late deliveries.
  • Provider quality drives dissatisfaction more than courier quality. Bad‑provider‑rating flags appear on 4.1 % of one‑timer orders, about 15 % higher than the other cohorts. Courier‑rating issues remain under 1.4 % everywhere, so provider‑side factors (packaging, prep time, item accuracy) are the bigger leverage.
  • Customer‑service tickets mirror the delay pattern. Timing‑quality tickets dominate each cohort but peak in one‑time churners (2.8 % vs 2.2 % baseline). Delivery‑quality and missing/wrong‑item tickets also climb for one‑timers, highlighting that the very first interaction exposes multiple pain points.
  • Price perception is unlikely the root cause. Average eater fees differ by just €0.14 between the worst and best cohorts. Operational pain—not fees—is the churn trigger.
